      STRATEGIC LANDSCAPE

      Guidance

      Vision

      A vision is an aspirational description of what an organisation would like to accomplish or achieve in the mid-term or long-term future. It is intended to serve as a clear guide for choosing current and future courses of action.

      Examples

      • Amazon's vision is to be earth's most customer centric company; to build a place where people can come to find and discover anything they might want to buy online.

      Test your vision

      • Is it unique to your organisation?
      • Is it clear to all the employees?
      • Is it well equipped to shape big and small decisions?

      Budget

      Budget is an allocated amount to achieve the business objectives over a specific future period of time.

      Test your budget

      • Is your budget adequate in terms of your defined objectives?

      Guidance

      Objective

      Business objectives are the outcomes that an organisation aims to achieve. Business strategies are created to achieve these outcomes.

      Examples

      • To exceed $10 million in the next ten years.
      • Expand sales to existing customers.

      Test your objectives

      • Are the objectives Specific?
      • Are the objectives Measurable?
      • Are the objectives Achievable?
      • Are the objectives Relevant?
      • What is the Timeline for these objectives?

      Strategic Drivers

      Strategic Drivers are the internal and external factors that influence your business objective.

      Examples

      • Change in Policy
      • Government Legislation
      • Market Data

      Function

      A function is a division of a business structure which an organisation uses to control and manage its resources and capabilities.

      Examples

      • Corporate Services
      • Finance and Accounting

      Test your functions

      • Do you have a well structured function?
      • Are your functions independent from each other?
      • Do you know what your new business function will do?

      Function-Objective-KPI mapping

      KPI Lens - These are the KPI categories under which different KPIs can be defined. The KPI lenses can be thought of as benefits that you want a particular business function to deliver in order to achieve a particular objective. There are a total of 9 KPI lenses classified as either Financial or Non-financial in nature.

      Financial

      • Cost Avoidance
      • Cost Reduction
      • Increase in Revenue

      Non-Financial

      • Risk Treatment
      • Increased Quality
      • Increased Client Satisfaction
      • Increased Employee and Stakeholder satisfaction
      • Reduction in Time
      • Legislative Compliance

      KPIs - Or Key Point Indicators, are the metrics using which an objective's success can be measured.

      In order to fill the KPIs, ask yourself this: For this function, if we want to achieve an objective, what needs to be done (KPI) so that there is "Increase in Revenue" or "Increase in client satisfaction" (KPI lens).

      In order to define KPIs:

      a) Select the business function

      b) Choose an Objective

      c) Select the KPI Lenses that you think are relevant for each business function for that objective

      d) Fill in the KPI for each of the selected lenses

      Explore

      Strategic Landscape

      You can explore your organisation's strategic landscape here - the organisation's vision, objectives, budget and business functions.

      Innovation Landscape

      The Innovation Landscape is a dashboard where you can browse all innovations submitted by function heads for each KPI. You can filter the KPIs by functions or objectives.

      By clicking on a KPI card, you can view the innovations posted by the function head for that KPI.
